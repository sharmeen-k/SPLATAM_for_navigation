Overview

This project is an experiment harness around SplaTAM (Splat, Track & Map 3D Gaussians for Dense RGB-D SLAM, Keetha et al., CVPR 2024). We do not re-implement the method; instead we drive the upstream system on our own data to evaluate how well Gaussian-Splatting SLAM holds up on footage relevant to robot navigation.

The work has three parts:

  1. Baseline reproduction on the Replica dataset (room0, office0) to validate our setup against published numbers.
  2. A data pipeline that converts Intel RealSense ROS 2 .mcap recordings into the NeRFCapture layout SplaTAM expects.
  3. Evaluation of full RGB-D SLAM (tracking + mapping, no ground-truth poses) on four custom sequences — both handheld and robot-mounted.

Pipeline

Footage is recorded on an Intel RealSense D4xx (640×480 color + depth) as ROS 2 .mcap bags. Our converter (mcap2dataset.py) turns a bag into a SplaTAM-ready dataset:

  1. Read color + depth intrinsics from the CameraInfo topics.
  2. Undistort the color image.
  3. Reproject depth into the color frame (identity extrinsic, Z-min collision policy).
  4. Rescale depth to SplaTAM's fixed scale (uint16 / 6553.5 = meters).
  5. Time-sync RGB & depth within a 30 ms window.
  6. Write rgb/, depth/, and transforms.json (identity poses — SplaTAM estimates them).

✓ Successful outcomes

On the desk and robot-mounted sequences, rendering quality is strong (seq2: PSNR 26.2, SSIM 0.91) and seq4 achieves the lowest trajectory error (ATE 26.5 cm). These confirm the pipeline produces usable maps when motion is moderate and depth is dense.

✗ Failure cases

The handheld sequences (seq1, seq2) show large trajectory drift (ATE up to ~210 cm) despite decent per-frame rendering. Fast hand motion plus sparse/noisy RealSense depth makes camera tracking unreliable without ground-truth poses. seq3 also has the weakest rendering (PSNR 15.8) — likely cause: TODO confirm (motion blur? texture-poor lab?).

Note: the pipeline also revealed practical issues — depth maps average only ~59% valid pixels on raw bags, and the Colab runtime repeatedly died mid-run, requiring checkpoint resumes.
